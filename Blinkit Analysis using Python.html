def univariate_numeric(col):
    plt.figure(figsize=(14,5))

    # Histogram + KDE
    plt.subplot(1,2,1)
    sns.histplot(df[col], bins=30, kde=True, palette="viridis", edgecolor="black")
    plt.title(f"Distribution of {col}", fontsize=14)
    plt.xlabel(col)
    plt.ylabel("Frequency")

    # Boxplot
    plt.subplot(1,2,2)
    sns.boxplot(x=df[col], palette="viridis")
    plt.title(f"Boxplot of {col}", fontsize=14)
    plt.xlabel(col)

    plt.show()

univariate_numeric("Sales")

Conclusion

A. The highest sales are observed in the following item categories:

Fruits and Vegetables (178,124) Snack Foods (175,434) Household (135,977) Frozen Foods (18,559) Dairy (101,276)

-This indicates that fresh produce and quick-consumption foods are driving significant revenue.

B. Outlets with the Highest Total Sales:

OUT035 (133,104) OUT046 (132,113) OUT013 (131,809)

-These outlets are major sales contributors and could be considered benchmark locations for best practices.

C. Sales by Region:

Tier 3: 472,133 Tier 2: 393,151 Tier 1: 336,398

-Outlets in Tier 3 locations are leading in overall sales, suggesting higher customer engagement or larger average basket size in these areas.

D. Sales by Establishment Year:

1998: 204,522 (highest) 2017: 133,104 2010: 132,113 2000: 131,809 2022: 131,478

-The outlets established in 1998 have the highest cumulative sales, indicating either a long-standing customer base or favorable location/demographics, indicating strong market potential. Consider expansion or replication of successful strategies from these outlets to others.

E. Fast-moving vs. Slow-moving Item Types:

Fast-movers: Fruits and Vegetables, Snack Foods, Household items (highest sales)

Slow-movers: Starchy Foods, Breakfast items, Seafood (lowest sales) Fast-movers represent core revenue streams, whereas slow-movers may require promotional support to increase sales.

-Allocate more inventory to fast-moving categories (Fruits and Vegetables, Snack Foods, Household) to prevent stockouts and maximize sales, while optimizing stock levels for slow-movers to reduce wastage.

-Launch targeted offers and discounts for slow-moving items (Starchy Foods, Breakfast, Seafood) to boost demand and clear stagnant inventory.
